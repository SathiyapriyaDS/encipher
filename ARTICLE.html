From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

In grammar, an article is any member of a class of dedicated words that are used with nouns to indicate the identifiability of the object. The category of articles constitutes a part of speech.

Articles combine with nouns to form noun phrases and typically specify grammatical definiteness. In English, the and a (or an) are the definite and indefinite articles respectively. Articles in many other languages also carry additional grammatical information such as gender, number, and case. Articles are part of a broader category called determiners, which also include demonstratives, possessive determiners, and quantifiers. In linguistic interlinear glossing, articles are abbreviated as ART.

Types of article

Definite article

"Definite article" redirects here. For the comedy album, see Definite Article.

A definite article is an article that marks a definite noun phrase. Definite articles such as the refer to a particular member of a group. It may be something that the speaker has already mentioned, or it may be something uniquely specified.

For example,, Sentence 1 uses the definite article and thus expresses a request for a particular book. In contrast, Sentence 2 uses an indefinite article and thus conveys that the speaker would be satisfied with any book.

  1. Give me the book.
  2. Give me a book.

The definite article can also be used in English to indicate a specific classes:

The cabbage white butterfly lays its eggs on members of the Brassica genus.
